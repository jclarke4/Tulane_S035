4-Step Response to an Opioid Overdose

1 of 1

In preventing overdose, it is important to be able to recognize overdose symptoms, administer naloxone if accessible, call for emergency medical services immediately, and provide rescue breaths until they regain normal breathing or EMS has arrived. If you are with someone who experiences an opioid overdose – REMEMBER NALOXONE ONLY WORKS FOR OPIOIDS NOT substances like COCAINE, SPEED, BENZOS, or Alcohol.

As pharmacy staff, you will find yourself educating customers on how to respond to an overdose and administer naloxone, you also may find yourself in the need to administer naloxone to someone. It is also important that you are familiar with the various product types and methods of administration.

If you think someone may be overdosing, try to wake them up or doing a sternal rub by making a fist and firmly rubbing against the center of the breastbone with you knuckles. The sternal rub, as it is officially called, is a noxious stimulus used to test someone’s level of consciousness. They have to be in a very deep coma before they are unable to respond, and how vigorously they do respond tells you how deeply unconscious they actually are.

CALL 911

It is important to report that the person’s breathing has slowed or stopped, if the person is unresponsive, and give the exact location.

ADMINISTER NALOXONE

Intermuscular

  1. Clean skin - though this is ideal, if cleaning supplies are not available, do not waste time searching for them
  2. Draw up naloxone in the syringe
  3. Inject into upper arm or thigh

ADMINISTER NALOXONE

Nasal Spray

  1. Remove from packaging
  2. Tilt the person’s head back and spray one nostril. Do not test this product as it has a single release point, so you do not want to waste it

Narcan

Luer Lock

ADMINISTER NALOXONE

Auto-injector (Evzio)

  1. Pull out of the case
  2. Remove red safety guard
  3. Press into the inner or outer thigh

BREATHE

  1. Lay the person on their back
  2. Make sure the airway is clear
  3. Use a protective face mask if available
  4. Tilt head back to open the airway
  5. Seal your mouth over their mouth, pinch the nose, and give deep breaths, making sure the chest rises. Repeat 1 breath every 5 seconds until revived or EMS arrives

CHECK THE PERSON

  1. If the person is still unresponsive after 2-3 minutes administer a second dose of naloxone
  2. Continue rescue breathing until the person is revived or EMS arrives

It is ideal to wait for EMS to arrive, however, if for some reason you leave, make sure to leave the empty naloxone doses on the ground in front of the person's face so that EMS will understand it was administered. If you must leave the person at all, even for a minute to phone 911, make sure you put them in the Recovery Position. Lay the person on his or her side, support his or her body with a bent knee, and the face turned to the side with the hand under the head supporting the head. This will help to keep their airway clear and prevent them from choking on their vomit if they begin to throw-up.
